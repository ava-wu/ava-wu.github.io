About

I'm a recent Berkeley graduate, interested in where we see biology have an impact on society, such as genetic privacy, forensics, and environmental health. I am also unabashedly enthusiastic about languages, having grown up in a multilingual household and community in Southern California. I'm fluent in French, and I have a basic understanding of Modern Greek and conversational Mandarin Chinese.

Currently, I am an Assistant Lab Manager and Project Coordinator for the Interdisciplinary Research Group on Privacy, at UC Berkeley. I manage the International Privacy Literacy Project, which bridges knowledge gaps between cultural and linguistic communities, specifically where the knowledge pertains to information privacy. I also assist the Executive Director with social media content for the Summer Institute in Computational Social Science - Howard / Mathematica.

Outside of the lab, I am an event supervisor and test writer for Science Olympiad. Plus, I'm involved in film and theatre as a producer, stage manager, writer, actor, and video editor. You can usually find me with a musical stuck in my head.
