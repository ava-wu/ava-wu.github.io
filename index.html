About

Hi, there, I'm Ava. I am a researcher and recent UC Berkeley graduate with diverse interests in science, language, law, and performing arts. The breadth of my interests stems from curiosity and a drive to take on new challenges. I'm interested in any issue that involves finding solutions, solving puzzles, and connecting with people--which can cover quite a lot.

Specifically, I am interested in where we see biology have an impact on society, such as genetic privacy, forensics, and environmental health. I am also unabashedly enthusiastic about languages, having grown up in a multilingual household and community.

Currently, I am an Assistant Lab Manager and Project Coordinator for the Interdisciplinary Research Group on Privacy, at UC Berkeley. I manage the International Privacy Literacy Project, which seeks to bridge knowledge gaps between cultural and linguistic communities, specifically as it pertains to knowledge about information privacy. I also assist the Executive Director with social media content for the Summer Institute in Computational Social Science - Howard / Mathematica.

Outside of the lab, I am an event supervisor and test writer for Science Olympiad. In my spare time, you can find me engaging in the performing arts.

Or Netflix and video games. I'm human too.
